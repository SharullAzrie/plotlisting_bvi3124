    Description

    Pahang, the largest of the 14 states in Malaysia, is renowned for its impressive size, interesting tourism activities, and breathtaking tropical rainforest. As a result, it is a popular destination for both local and international tourists, with Kuantan being one of its must-visit locations.

    Kuantan, the largest state capital on the East Coast of Malaysia, is not only rich in ecotourism opportunities, but its unique position overlooking the South China Sea adds to its appeal and makes it a favorite among travelers.

    If you're planning a trip to Kuantan and are on a budget, but still looking for delicious and affordable dining options, we are here to help. Our website features a list of the best places to eat in Kuantan, with unbeatable prices and delicious food that is sure to satisfy your taste buds. Join us on this culinary adventure!


    1. Gelora Steak House
    2. Kimstone Foodcourt & Hall
    3. Restaurant Mint
    4. Nasi Ulam D'Pondok
    5. KarRatz Cafe
    6. Seni Bidadara Pizza


    Did you know?

    Kuantan is famous for its delicious seafood, particularly its grilled seafood. Visitors can find many seafood restaurants along the beach offering fresh seafood that is caught daily. Another popular local dish in Kuantan is "nasi dagang," a rice dish made with coconut milk and served with a variety of curries and side dishes.

    General Info


    1. Gelora Steak House

    Discover a world of delicious Western cuisine in Kuantan at Gelora Steak House. With a focus on providing high-quality food at reasonable prices, this restaurant offers a simple and relaxed atmosphere for you to enjoy your meal.At Gelora Steak House, steak is their specialty and they offer a wide variety of cuts cooked to perfection. Whether you prefer rib-eye, sirloin, or filet, you'll be able to find a steak that suits your taste.In addition to steaks, they also offer other Western dishes such as chicken chop, lamb chop, and salmon, as well as a selection of pasta, pizza, and lasagna options. And for the little ones, they have a children's menu available. So, gather your family and head over to Gelora Steak House for an unforgettable dining experience.

    2. Kimstone Foodcourt & Hall
    Located in Berserah, discover a unique dining experience at Kimstone, a food court set in a park-like setting. With its stylish ambiance, this food court not only offers a variety of food stalls serving popular dishes like coconut shakes from Kelapa Joget and satays from Satezul, but also provides guests with comfortable council seating under a beautiful white canopy. Discover a range of cuisine from stalls offering Western food, claypot dishes, and hot and fried dishes from Sarathai and more. This is a must-visit spot for food lovers, with a special outdoor dining area lit up by lights that create a garden-like atmosphere. Don't miss out on this new and exciting addition to the Malaysian culinary scene!

    3. Restaurant Mint

    Experience a relaxing and romantic dining atmosphere with your loved ones at Mint Kuantan Restaurant. Nestled on the edge of Balok Beach, this restaurant offers a spacious dining area and a cool breeze deck that creates a comfortable and intimate ambiance.Savor delicious western cuisine while enjoying quality time with your partner, family or friends. Ideal for families, large groups or romantic dinners, Mint Kuantan Restaurant boasts a spacious and peaceful atmosphere. For those seeking a beach wedding, Mint Kuantan Restaurant also provides beach wedding services and event venues. Book your special day now and make your beach wedding dreams come true!

    4. Nasi ulam D'Pondok

    Savor the intense aroma of tempoyak-cooked catfish and the delicious taste of fresh catfish mixed with tempoyak when you visit Pahang. This popular and mouthwatering cuisine is sure to leave you excited. For a true taste of Pahang's local flavors, visit Nasi Ulam D'Pondok in Kuantan. With its strategic location opposite Bukit Setongkol Mosque, this restaurant attracts a large crowd after the Jumaat prayer. Be sure to plan your visit accordingly. Discover a feast for your taste buds with Nasi Ulam D'Pondok's extensive selection of side dishes, including over 75 options served every day. Watch as the tantalizing aroma of traditional kampung cuisine fills the dining room, making it hard to resist the delicious spread of options. Treat yourself to dishes such as Ikan Siakap Stim, Gulai Lemak Daging Salai, Tempoyak Ubi Ikan Bilis, Ikan Keli Berlada, Tilapia Goreng Masam Manis, and Ikan Patin Goreng Berlada. Pair your meal with over 20 types of vegetable dishes to complete your dining experience. For a true taste of Pahang, visit Nasi Ulam D'Pondok, open from 7.00am to 5.00pm for traditional dishes and ulam-ulaman, and 6.00pm to 12.00am for western cuisine.

    5. KarRatz Cafe

    Kuantan is a city thriving with hipster-style cafes that are in high demand among the locals. Despite the growing number of cafes with this concept, KarRatz Café remains a popular destination, particularly for fans of classic cars and motorcycles. At KarRatz Café, you'll find a menu filled with Western cuisine, each dish named after old trains and train accessories. This unique touch adds to the dining experience for classic car and motorcycle enthusiasts, making it the perfect spot for a meal with friends who share the same interests.

    6. Seni Bidadara Pizza

    Experience a touch of Italy in Kuantan with Seni Bidadara Pizza. This restaurant serves up a variety of mouth-watering Italian cuisine and is known for its creative menu names and relaxed dining atmosphere. From XXL pizzas to deep dish pizzas and even charcoal pizzas, each dish is prepared using traditional charcoal ovens to give the perfect smoky flavor that'll transport you to the streets of Italy. Whether you're in the mood for pizza or Italian plates, Seni Bidadara Pizza is the perfect spot to indulge in a unique and delicious dining experience.

    Description

    Public transportation in Kuantan, Pahang is fairly well-developed and provides a range of options for visitors and residents. Some of the main types of public transportation in the area include:
    1. Rapid Kuantan (MyRapid)
    2. Taxi
    3. Grab Car

    Did you know?

    Kuantan has a rich history of transportation, starting with waterways in the Neolithic era and later expanding to roads and bridges in the 19th century. Today, the city continues to expand its transportation network, offering a well-developed public transportation system with buses and taxis, a light rail system connecting the city center to the airport, and a bicycle rental service. These options make it easy and convenient for visitors to explore the historic and vibrant city.

    General Info


    1. Rapid Bus
    Kuantan has a Rapid Bus service that provides fast and convenient transportation within the city and to other parts of the state. Buses are frequent and offer a cost-effective way to get around.

    2. Taxis
    Taxis are readily available in Kuantan and are a convenient way to get around the city. They are either metered or operated on a flat-rate basis.

    3. Grab Car
    Grab Car is a popular ride-hailing service that allows users to book private rides with a few taps on their smartphone. It offers a convenient and flexible option for getting around Kuantan and its surroundings.

    Overall, public transportation in Kuantan, Pahang is well-developed and offers a range of options to meet the needs of visitors and residents. Whether you prefer a fast and efficient bus service, a taxi, or a private ride, there is something to suit everyone's needs.

    Description

    Kuantan, the capital of Pahang, offers a variety of shopping options for visitors and locals alike. From modern malls like Kuantan City Mall, East Coast Mall, Berjaya Megamall, and Kuantan Parade, to traditional markets like Serambi Teruntum, there are plenty of places to find what you're looking for. For those looking for unique, locally-made products, the Car Boot Sale at Taman Kerang is a must-visit. Whether you're in the market for clothes, toys, food, or souvenirs, you're sure to find something to your taste in Kuantan.

    Did you know?

    Kuantan has a rich history of shopping, from traditional street markets to modern malls. In the past, street markets were key centers for trade and social interaction. Today, visitors can still enjoy the traditional street markets, which offer a unique glimpse into the city's history and culture, alongside modern malls. Kuantan provides a range of shopping options that reflect its history and vibrant culture.

    General Info


    1. Kuantan City Mall

    Kuantan City Mall is a modern and spacious shopping center located in the central commercial district of Kuantan. This new and exciting mall offers a comprehensive shopping, dining, and entertainment experience, making it a popular destination for families and visitors alike. The mall features a variety of shops, restaurants, play areas, and entertainment options, ensuring there is something for everyone. With its convenient location and world-class amenities, Kuantan City Mall provides a memorable and enriching experience for all who visit. Whether you're looking for a fun day out with family, or simply want to indulge in some retail therapy, this mall is a must-visit destination that promises to exceed your expectations.

    2. East Coast Mall

    East Coast Mall is a modern and vibrant shopping center located in the heart of Kuantan city, in Pahang. It is conveniently situated adjacent to Kuantan City Mall and provides a unique shopping, dining, and entertainment experience for visitors. The mall boasts a diverse mix of domestic and international retailers, offering a wide range of shopping options from fashion to entertainment, arts and culture. In addition, the mall features a variety of local and international cuisine options, providing visitors with a delicious and culturally diverse dining experience. As a family lifestyle center, East Coast Mall is designed to cater to the needs and interests of all visitors, making it the perfect destination for a fun day out.

    3. Berjaya Megamall

    For years, this location has been a go-to spot for finding unique items and second-hand goods, or "bric-a-brac," for the people of Kuantan. However, with the emergence of newer bric-a-brac centers, it has seen a decline in popularity. Despite this, it remains a cherished destination for those who enjoy watching puppet shows and dining at fast food restaurants. Despite the rise of newer options, this location still holds a special place in the hearts of its loyal patrons.

    4. Kuantan Parade

    Kuantan Parade is a top-notch shopping mall located in the city of Kuantan, Pahang, Malaysia. It offers a diverse mix of retail shops, dining options, and entertainment venues, featuring well-known department stores, trendy fashion boutiques, an array of electronics stores, and a large food court. The mall also features modern cinemas, a children's play area, and recreational facilities, making it a complete shopping and entertainment destination for visitors of all ages. Throughout the year, the mall hosts exciting events and promotions, including seasonal sales, fashion shows, and live performances, and its modern and well-maintained facilities, including air-conditioning, provide a comfortable and enjoyable experience for visitors.

    5. Serambi Teruntum

    Serambi Teruntum is a new and exciting destination in Kuantan, offering a diverse range of products and goods to visitors. This vibrant marketplace is a hub for food products, textiles, crafts, seafood, local and international produce, and so much more. Open daily from 9.00am to 10.00pm, Serambi Teruntum is a convenient and accessible destination for anyone looking to explore the local culture and commerce of Kuantan. With its wide selection of products and its bustling atmosphere, Serambi Teruntum is a must-visit location for anyone looking for unique and authentic experiences in Kuantan.

    6. Car Boot Sale Taman Kerang

    Taman Kerang in Kuantan, Pahang is a popular destination for car boot sales. It offers a range of products including clothing, toys, snacks, and meals such as rice and laksa. Coffee stalls are also available, including a special blend called Kopi Gonchang, as well as fruit drinks. A popular food stall offers giant karipap at an affordable price, and there is also a stall selling drinks with grass jelly, including a large glass for RM3. The area is pleasant at night and there is a designated eating area. Visitors are encouraged to maintain its cleanliness by properly disposing of their trash.

    Description

    Travelling in Pahang, Malaysia offers a unique experience, with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions and modern amenities. Some popular destinations include Teluk Cempedak Beach, Berkelah Falls, Menara Kuantan 188, Air Terjun Sungai Pandan, Natural Batik Village, and Bukit Panorama Sungai Lembing. Visitors can indulge in outdoor activities, visit historical sites, and sample delicious local cuisine while enjoying the beauty of Pahang.

    Did you know

    Kuantan is a popular travel destination known for its stunning beaches, lush rainforests, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant nightlife. It offers a variety of attractions including surf spots, hiking opportunities, and historical sites, making it a destination for nature and culture enthusiasts alike. Visitors can also enjoy local street food and experience the city's unique atmosphere.

    General Info


    1. Pantai Teluk Chempedak

    Teluk Chempedak is a beach in Kuantan, Pahang, Malaysia. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, offering a scenic and sandy shoreline, clear waters, and opportunities for recreation and relaxation. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. The beach is also surrounded by lush green forests, making for a beautiful and serene environment. There may also be facilities available such as restrooms, food stalls, and souvenir shops. Overall, Teluk Chempedak is a beautiful and fun destination for those looking to escape to the beach and enjoy some rest and recreation.

    2. Berkelah Falls

    Berkelah Falls is a waterfall located in Kuantan, Pahang, Malaysia. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals, offering scenic beauty and opportunities for recreation and relaxation. The waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and a tranquil environment, making for a peaceful and serene atmosphere. Visitors can hike through the forest to reach the waterfall, and take a refreshing dip in the cool and clear pool below. There may also be picnic areas and restrooms available for visitors to use. Overall, Berkelah Falls is a beautiful destination for those looking to escape to nature and enjoy some rest and recreation.

    3. Menara Kuantan 188

    Menara Kuantan 188 is a tower located in Kuantan, Pahang, Malaysia. It is a relatively new landmark in the city, and offers a unique and modern take on a traditional tower structure. The tower is known for its observation deck and revolving restaurant, offering panoramic views of the surrounding city and coast. Visitors can ascend to the top of the tower to take in the views and enjoy a meal in the restaurant. There may also be additional amenities, such as a gift shop or information center, available for visitors. Menara Kuantan 188 is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, offering a unique experience and breathtaking views of Kuantan.

    4. Air Terjun Sungai Pandan

    Air Terjun Sungai Pandan is a waterfall located in Pahang, Malaysia. It is a popular destination for those who enjoy hiking and exploring natural beauty. The waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and has a pool below where visitors can swim and cool off. There may also be picnic areas and restrooms available for visitors to use. Access to the waterfall may involve a hike through the forest, offering opportunities to take in the scenic beauty of the area. Overall, Air Terjun Sungai Pandan is a beautiful destination for those looking to escape to nature and enjoy a refreshing swim.

    5. Natural Batik Village

    The Natural Batik Village is a cultural and craft center located in Pahang, Malaysia. It is dedicated to preserving and promoting the traditional art of batik-making. Visitors can observe skilled artisans creating intricate designs and patterns using the traditional wax-resist dyeing technique. There may also be workshops and demonstrations available for those who wish to learn more about this traditional art form. The Natural Batik Village is a great destination for those who appreciate local culture and traditional craftsmanship. In addition to observing batik-making, visitors may also have the opportunity to purchase handmade batik products as gifts or souvenirs.

    6. Bukit Panorama Sungai Lembing

    Bukit Panorama Sungai Lembing is a scenic viewpoint located in Pahang, Malaysia. It offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including forests and valleys. The viewpoint is located on top of a hill and may be accessible by car or a short hike. From the top, visitors can take in the natural beauty of the area and snap photos of the breathtaking views. Bukit Panorama Sungai Lembing is a popular destination for those who enjoy scenic vistas and outdoor adventures. There may also be picnic areas or other amenities available for visitors to use. Overall, Bukit Panorama Sungai Lembing is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the beauty of Pahang's natural landscapes.
